Getting There

Your complete guide on how to reach our island paradise.

Travel Info

By Air

The nearest airport is Surigao Airport (SUG) on Mindanao, with flights from Manila, Cebu and Davao. Butuan Airport (BAG) is larger; from Butuan one drives ~2–3h to Surigao City. Domestic carriers (e.g. Philippines AirAsia, Cebu Pacific, PAL Express) serve these airports.

By Sea / Ferry

From Surigao City, several ferry operators run daily trips to Dinagat Islands. Major ports include San Jose (Dinagat), Loreto, and Cagdianao. Travel time varies from 1–3 hours depending on the destination and vessel type (fast ferry or RORO). Companies like FastCat and local operators offer regular schedules; check their websites or local port listings for the latest timetables and fares.

Local Transportation

Within Dinagat, there is a circumferential highway (mostly paved) linking San Jose, Loreto, Libjo, Cagdianao, Basilisa; expect some rough sections. Motorbikes (“habal-habal”) and jeepneys connect towns. From San Jose to Basilisa (north coast) is ~2.5h by jeepney.
